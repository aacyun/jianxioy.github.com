Hi, I'm Daryl.

I live to learn.

I am a computer scientist who graduated from Carnegie Mellon University. I am a polyglot programmer and enjoy building cool stuff that helps people.

I love entrepreneurship, technology, computer games and used to be a professional gamer. Most of all, I love all types of sports. I enjoy working out at the gym, recreational gymnastics, soccer, snowboarding and competitive floorball. I am also an ardent fan of Liverpool Football Club and a hobbyist photographer.

On a typical day you can find me drinking a cup of home brewed black coffee reading, writing code and listening to trance music. I email from daryl [at] darylyeo [dot] com and would love to hear from you. Feel free to hit me up on the social networks (my username is almost always jianxioy). You can find me on Facebook, Twitter, GitHub, Flickr, LinkedIn, Instagram, Google+, Forrst, Quora, Pinboard.

I wish to utilize my knowledge I have gained in my life to make the world a better place. Currently, I am a work in progress and here is my story.


Personal.

I was born in Singapore to two great parents and I am the eldest of three. I grew up and went to school at Maris Stella High School, St. Joseph's Institution and Raffles Junior College.

During this time, I was involved mainly in gymnastics and professional gaming. I was also a student's councillor during my junior college days. I picked up simple scripting and decided that I wanted to learn how to make games so I naively set my mind to study computer science in college.

Before I went to college, I served my National Service as a police officer in the Singapore Police Force. As a police officer, I saw the best and worst sides of people. There were also some things which I wish I never saw. It was an experience I will never forget and I am thankful for it because it played a huge part in molding me into the person I am today.

My siblings and I during my graduation from the police academy.

As I was serving my National Service, I managed to obtain a full overseas scholarship from the Infocomm Development Authority of Singapore to read Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University.

At Carnegie Mellon University, I made many new friends and learnt a lot from countless talented people. I took part in hackathons, founded the CMU Floorball Club and joined the Delta Tau Delta fraternity. After completing my undergraduate degree, I stayed at Carnegie Mellon University and completed a Master of Information Systems Management. My time in college was a very humbling and fruitful experience. I am now currently residing in San Francisco.



Computer Scientist.

At Carnegie Mellon University, I learnt that CS was not just about making games or programming. There were deep mathematical principles that formed the bedrock of most, if not all, CS fundamentals. Algorithms, systems programming, human-computer interaction, programming languages, etc., were just some of the few fields I barely scraped the surface of. It opened my eyes to the world of CS and how these big ideas and theories were used to solve some of the world's biggest problems.

My parents and I with my advisor, Prof. Mark Stehlik, at my college graduation.

It was also here where I met so many smart people whom I learnt and grew from. I have since changed my ambition to be a game designer and instead intend to utilize the knowledge and experiences I gained in college to make a dent in the universe.



Polyglot Programmer.

I am familiar with multiple programming languages but recently I have fallen in love with the elegance and beauty of scripting languages Ruby and Python. I hope to get involved in more Ruby and Python projects in the near future. Right now, though, I'm toying with game development. For a look at some things I am working on, head over to my projects page or my GitHub profile. For a more thorough look at my skills, feel free to peruse my résumé.



Professional Gamer.

During my teenage years, I was a professional gamer. I played first-person shooters, namely the Quake series and the Unreal series. I had most success in the latter. This experience showed me what camaraderie and working as a team was like and I had some of my best experiences with my clan. We were one of the top teams in our country and even went a season undefeated. Of course, winning cash playing Unreal Tournament was a lot of fun.
